# Royal Card Prompts

These prompts define four standalone royal card-face concepts. They are not runtime card replacements unless a future task explicitly wires them into card data.

## Shared Rules

Create finished fantasy royal card-face art in a strict vertical 3:4 aspect ratio, suitable as a complete card face. Include an integrated ornate royal gold frame visible on all sides, with polished gemstone accents and courtly trim. Keep one clear centered focal subject, readable at card size. No readable text, no numbers, no UI icons, no watermark.

## royal-trophy-3pts

A sovereign victory card worth 3 points, centered on a magnificent royal trophy on a raised marble dais inside a grand coronation hall. The trophy is gold, pearl, and crystal, surrounded by banners, laurel motifs, soft throne-room light, and high-prestige royal ceremony. No extra-turn, privilege, or theft symbolism.

## royal-scepter-2pts-extra-turn

A 2-point royal scepter card with extra-turn symbolism. Show a jeweled royal scepter floating above a ceremonial clockwork dais, wrapped in looping golden light trails, repeated circular arcs, and mirrored return paths that clearly suggest another turn, momentum, and royal command.

## royal-scroll-2pts-privilege

A 2-point royal scroll card with privilege symbolism. Show a sealed royal decree scroll open on a gilded lectern in a court archive, with wax seals, silk ribbons, palace columns, keys of office, and soft sovereign light. The scroll must read as authority, permission, and court-granted privilege, without readable writing.

## royal-saber-2pts-steal

A 2-point royal saber card with steal symbolism. Show a jeweled royal saber cutting through the light above a treasury pedestal while a covert gloved hand or shadowed court agent seizes a precious relic. The scene should feel royal and illicit at once: gold vault, velvet cushion, scattered gems, predatory motion, no gore.
