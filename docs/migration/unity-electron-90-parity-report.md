# Unity Electron 90% Scoped Parity Report

Date: 2026-05-09

## Scope

This report defines the current Unity target as 90% scoped parity with the Electron local PvP
surface, not a full Electron replacement. The scope is:

- local PvP board/market/royal/player-rail presentation;
- visible guided actions through the committed full-coverage local PvP replay;
- normal take-gems clicks on any legal non-gold board line, with board and inventory state updating
  immediately between replay checkpoints;
- Unity-side replay parity and Editor/CLI visual evidence;
- LocalDev platform services only.

Out of scope for this 90% mark:

- arbitrary non-fixture local PvP rules without replay checkpoints;
- final card art, animations, audio, overlays, settings chrome, online/LAN flows, and release-store
  SDK behavior;
- Steamworks.NET, Epic Online Services, app IDs, product IDs, partner tooling, upload flows, or
  live SDK callbacks.

## Remaining 10%

The next work should not claim full Electron replacement until these are done:

1. Replace guided fixture progression with arbitrary local PvP reducer behavior that does not depend
   on checkpoints for normal player turns.
2. Add real card preview, buy/reserve confirmation, cancel, reserve-gold, bonus, steal, discard, and
   royal-selection interaction states instead of only highlighted guided targets.
3. Bring over final card art, gem art, animation timing, audio, surface themes, and app chrome only
   after reducer independence is green.
4. Keep Steam/Epic SDKs deferred until replay parity, non-fixture local PvP, Windows build
   repeatability, and platform smoke-test plans are all green.
